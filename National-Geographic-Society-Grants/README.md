## Grant Overview

**Program:** National Geographic Society Grants
**Funding Levels:**
- **Level I Grants:** Up to $20,000 (1-year projects, 2 years for technology-focused)
- **Level II Grants:** $50,000-$100,000+ (2-year projects)
- **Young Explorers Grants (YEG):** $2,000-$5,000 (ages 18-25)

**Focus Areas:**
- Ocean
- Land
- Wildlife
- Human Histories and Cultures
- Human Ingenuity

**Eligibility:** Individuals pursuing impactful work in science, conservation, storytelling, education, and technology

---

## What the Grants Offer

- **Flexible funding** for field-based exploration and storytelling
- **Support for travel** and research expenses
- **Recognition as National Geographic Explorer**
- **Access to National Geographic network and resources
- **Potential for media collaboration** (articles, films, social media features)

---

## Eligibility

**Level I Grants (Early-Career):**
- Early-career individuals building networks
- Up to $20,000 for 1-year projects
- Focus on innovative approaches and initial exploration

**Level II Grants (Established):**
- Established professionals with track record
- $50,000-$100,000+ for 2-year projects
- Focus on advanced storytelling with significant impact

**Young Explorers Grants:**
- Ages 18-25
- $2,000-$5,000 for research, conservation, exploration
- Focus on emerging talent in humanities, social sciences, journalism/media, education

**General Requirements:**
- No advanced degree required
- Prior experience in research/exploration needed
- Projects must align with focus areas
- Competitive review (~1,200 applications for Level I, ~20 awarded)
