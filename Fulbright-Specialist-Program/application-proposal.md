**Question:** Please describe why you are interested in serving as a Fulbright Specialist and how your participation would benefit a foreign institution as well as your institution or employer in the United States.

**Response (Max 1500 words):**

As a children's literature author and educational designer with over 15 years of experience in instructional leadership, I am compelled to serve as a Fulbright Specialist to bridge cultures through storytelling and education. My project, Alexandria's World, centers an African American toddler exploring diverse cultures through authentic journeys aligned with National History Educational Standards for culture education. This work embodies the Fulbright mission of promoting mutual understanding through educational and cultural exchange.

Participating as a Fulbright Specialist would benefit foreign institutions by providing expertise in developing culturally responsive children's literature and educational materials. I can lead workshops on integrating culturally diverse stories into K-12 curricula, design curriculum modules that teach global citizenship and peace education, and collaborate with local educators to document and celebrate their cultural heritage. My approach combines rigorous educational design with authentic cultural storytelling, creating resources that are both pedagogically sound and culturally authentic.

My participation would also benefit Alexandria's Design, my U.S.-based women-owned small business, by expanding our international portfolio and demonstrating the global impact of our educational design work. Fulbright recognition would validate our approach to culturally responsive education, opening doors for future international collaborations and funding opportunities. Additionally, the 2-6 week Fulbright Specialist projects would provide immersive research opportunities to develop authentic Alexandria's World books, directly informing our publications and educational products that serve diverse audiences in the United States.

Most importantly, Fulbright Specialist service aligns with my personal mission: to create a pipeline of global citizens through children's literature that teaches cultural understanding, peace education, and the value of diversity. I am committed to using my expertise in educational leadership and children's literature to foster cross-cultural empathy and provide educators with resources that prepare young people for an interconnected world.

**Question:** Please describe any previous experience working in an international environment and why you believe that you would be effective serving as a Fulbright Specialist at an institution abroad. If you do not have prior experience in an international setting, please describe what strategies you would employ in order to be successful while serving as a Fulbright Specialist in a cross-cultural context.

**Response (Max 1500 words):**

While I have extensive experience with culturally diverse student populations in the United States, I have not yet worked extensively in international educational settings. However, I am actively developing strategies to ensure success as a Fulbright Specialist in cross-cultural contexts.

First, my approach is rooted in deep cultural humility and listening. As Principal Investigator on NSF-funded STEM education projects, I have collaborated with diverse research teams from varied cultural backgrounds. This experience has taught me that effective cross-cultural collaboration begins with respectful inquiry, not presumption of expertise. When working abroad, I would prioritize learning from local educators, cultural practitioners, and community members, treating their knowledge as primary and my role as facilitator rather than authority.

Second, I would employ my expertise in universal design for learning — creating educational materials that transcend cultural and linguistic barriers. This approach, which I have honed through Alexandria's Design's work with diverse U.S. school districts, ensures that resources are accessible across cultural contexts. My background in instructional design provides frameworks for adapting content to local needs while maintaining pedagogical integrity.

Third, I would leverage children's literature as a bridge. Storytelling is a universal human experience, and my work with Alexandria's World demonstrates how cultural exploration through a child's lens creates emotional connections across differences. This approach would allow me to engage with international communities through shared human experiences — the love of stories, the desire to teach children, and the commitment to cultural preservation — rather than imposing external frameworks.

Finally, I am prepared to adapt quickly and flexibly. My experience managing complex grant-funded projects with multiple stakeholders has developed my ability to pivot in response to changing circumstances and emergent needs. I understand that cross-cultural work requires flexibility, patience, and openness to unexpected learning opportunities.

Through these strategies — cultural humility, universal design, storytelling as bridge, and adaptive flexibility — I am confident in my ability to serve effectively as a Fulbright Specialist, contributing meaningfully to host institutions while learning from their rich cultural contexts and educational traditions.
